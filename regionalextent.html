Pan-Arctic & Regional Extent Forecasts

The forecasts below were initialized up to 2 months prior to present day.

We have forecasts initialized earlier and can make a custom set of figures upon request.

(Click on below buttons to view individual regions or scroll down)

Red stars show each initialization time. Each line represents a single model ensemble. We have regridded all model output to a common grid, but applied no further post processing. Some forecasts are bias corrected before we acquired them.

*Contains modified Copernicus Climate Change Service information 2018-2019, referring to ECMWF-YOPP*, ECMWF-C3S* and UKMO-C3S* models. Neither the European Commission nor ECMWF is responsible for any use that may be made of the Copernicus information or data it contains

Some models appear to have a large bias at the initialization time, which in most cases is because their land-sea mask is quite different than the obaservations. Scanning through the regional maps can help locate the region where the bias is greatest.

Two "benchmark" forecasts are given based purely upon observations. The Climatological Trend is an extrapolation of the long term trend for each day. The Damped Anomaly is the persistence of the anomaly starting on days ~30 days apart, damped into the future and added to the climatological trend.
